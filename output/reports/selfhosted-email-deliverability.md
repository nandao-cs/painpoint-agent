## Entrepreneurial fit
- **TAM signal:** email is universal; a steady stream of SMBs, MSPs, devs and privacy/sovereignty-driven orgs are leaving Google Workspace / M365 over price hikes, weak support, and data residency — but self-hosting email is brutally hard (deliverability, spam, SPF/DKIM/DMARC, IP reputation). The wedge: **self-host-grade control without the deliverability pain** (managed/hybrid).
- **Who would buy:** SMB IT & MSPs running client mail; EU orgs under data-sovereignty pressure; product teams sending transactional/app email who hate Postmark/SES lock-in.
- **Why now:** Workspace/M365 price increases + AI-feature bundling; Google/Yahoo 2024 bulk-sender authentication rules raised the bar on deliverability (paradoxically increasing the value of *managed* deliverability); rising EU sovereignty demand.

## Risks / why it might NOT be solvable
- **Incumbents own distribution + the bundle.** Workspace/M365 = email *plus* docs/calendar/meet; standalone email is a hard sell against the suite.
- **Deliverability is a multi-year moat** (IP/domain reputation) — hard to bootstrap, easy to get blocklisted.
- **Crowded "better email" niche:** Fastmail, Migadu, Mailbox.org, Proton, Purelymail already serve privacy/control buyers.
- **Thin margins + abuse/spam liability** at scale. Migration friction (calendar/contacts/shared drives) keeps users locked in.
- *Why nobody's "cracked" it:* network effects + the suite bundle, not a lack of demand — so the durable play is a **niche/sovereignty or MSP-channel** angle, not head-on with Google/Microsoft.
